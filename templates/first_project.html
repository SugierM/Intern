This dataset pulls job postings from Google's search results for Data Analyst positions in the United States.
Data collection is updated daily, starting from 4.11.2023. Collected on 28.12.2023.

Chart suggests a job market that highly values data-related skills, from foundational database management with SQL to data visualization with Power BI and Tableau, to statistical analysis with R and SAS, as well as the ability to present findings using tools like PowerPoint.

With nearly 20,000 mentions, SQL is by far the most in-demand technology for Data Analyst positions. This indicates that proficiency in SQL is likely essential for data analysts, as SQL is commonly used for querying and managing databases.
Excel is the second most listed skill, with over 13,000 mentions. This suggests that despite the prevalence of advanced analytical tools, Excel's versatility and widespread use keep it relevant in the data analysis field.
Python is the third most listed skill, showing its significant role in data analysis for scripting, data manipulation, and analysis tasks. The presence of R, although less frequent than Python, emphasizes the importance of statistical analysis skills in the data analyst job market.
PowerPoint being on the list, albeit the least mentioned, highlights that data analysts are expected to have the ability to present findings clearly and effectively, often using presentation software.
Power BI and Tableau are close in mentions, indicating that skills in business intelligence and data visualization tools are highly sought after by employers. The ability to not only analyze data but also present it in an accessible format is crucial.

There are two notable spikes in job offers for those skills, occurring around January and September. Towards the end of the collection period, there is a noticeable decrease in job offers for those particular skills. This decline might suggest a change in technology trends or could be related to seasonal variations.

PowerPoint and SAS show less fluctuation and lower overall frequency, which may indicate a more niche or stable demand for these skills without significant growth or decline in the timeframe shown.

LinkedIn and Upwork are the top platforms for job listings in this dataset, with other sites like BeBee, Trabajo.org and ZipRecruiter following behind. The remaining sites have significantly fewer job listings, suggesting they might serve niche markets or have a smaller user base for job postings.

The majority of internships do not specify a particular schedule type. Among those that do, most are for full-time internships. Part-time or temporary work internships are less commonly sought after by employers.
